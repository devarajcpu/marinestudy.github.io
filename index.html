Scavenge Fire

  • Indications
  • Action
  • Reasons
  • Prevention

Indications:

For small Fire,
  1. Increase in scavege temperature followed by incease in exhaust temp.
  2. Turbocharger surging followed by rpm fluctuations.

For large Fire,

  1. Red hottness of the scavenge reciever and paint blisters on the reciever wall.
  2. Smoke coming out from scavenge drains.

Actions:

  1. Infrom bridge, take control over to E/R, slow down the engine.
  2. Increase the cylinder lubrication feed rate if it's small fire.
  3. Keep close the scavenge drains.
  4. Cut-off the particular unit if required.
  5. Keep ready the nearby fire extinguishing system or prepare fixed fire fighting system if available(Either CO2 or Steam System).
  6. Let the fire smother by itself and confirm all parameters coming under the limit.
  7. Stop the engine for inspection-
    • Follow Enclosed space entry checklist.
    • Prepare and follow risk assesment.
    • Carry out inspection of,
      1. Free Movement of piston.
      2. Condition of piston rings and liner.
      3. Condition of piston.
      4. Condition of relief valve of the reciever.
      5. Condition of scavenge space.
      6. Condition of the diapragm.
      7. Injector condition and testing.
      8. Conditon of crosshead guides and shoes.

Reasons:

There are many reasons for scavenge fire, but mainly
  1. Blow-past due to broken or sticky piston rings.
  2. Improper fuel injection system leads to over heating of piston or damage to liner by after burning, late ignition, deep penatrisation, improper atomization.
  3. Excessive cylinder lubrication leads to accumulation of heavy oil deposits in scavenge space.
  4. Defective stuffing box will allow c/case oil to pass to the scavege space.
  5. Insufficient cooling of piston will also leads to piston over-heating.
  6. Back-Pressure due to turbocharger surging in case of over loading or sudden varying of loads.

Precautions/Preventions:

  1. Carry out scavenge space inspections on regular basis.
  2. Keep Record and monitor of scavenge drains.
  3. Maintian injectors as per the pms.
  4. Carry out Unit overhaul as per PMS.

Safety Devices Fitted to avoid scavenge fire:

  1. Scavenge high temperature alarm.
  2. Self closing Relief valve mounted on the reciever.
  3. Fixed fire fighting system CO2, DCP or Steam.

Scavenge space inspection:

Following are the items to check during scavenge space inspection-
  1. T/C flap condition and movement.
  2. Piston ring condition by pressing with brass rod.
  3. Piston ring groove conditon for carbon deposition.
  4. Condition of Piston Crown. Any marks of oil droplets indicates dripping of the injectors. This may lead to crown crack.
  5. Liner condition for abrasion or scuffing.
  6. Downside of piston for any oil leaks which indicates broken o-ring inside.
  7. Locking wire of the piston foot and crown.
  8. Clear scavenge drains.
  9. Condition of sludge. May find some small metal particles.
  10. Sludge level inside the space.
  11. Condition of relief valve.
